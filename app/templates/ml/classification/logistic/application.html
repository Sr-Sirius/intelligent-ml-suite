Logistic Regression - Application

Problem Description

This application aims to predict the risk of a car accident based on driver behavior and vehicle-related factors. By analyzing patterns such as average speed, number of trips, and driving time, the model classifies whether a driver is at high risk or low risk of being involved in an accident.

Dataset Explanation

The dataset contains information related to driving behavior and vehicle usage. It includes variables such as speed, number of trips, and driving time, which are used to identify patterns associated with accident risk.

Input Variables and Target Variable

  • Input variables: average speed, number of trips, driving time
  • Target variable: accident risk (1 = high risk, 0 = low risk)

Data Preparation

The dataset is preprocessed by cleaning missing values, normalizing numerical variables, and splitting the data into training and testing sets to evaluate model performance.

Model Training Process

A Logistic Regression model is trained using labeled data, where each record indicates whether a driver is high risk or low risk. The model learns relationships between driving behavior and accident risk.

Interpretation of Results

The model demonstrates strong performance in identifying high-risk drivers, achieving an accuracy of approximately 86%. The high recall (94%) indicates that most risky drivers are correctly detected, which is critical in safety-related applications where missing a high-risk case can have serious consequences.

The precision (89%) shows that the majority of drivers classified as high risk are indeed risky, although some false positives still occur. This reflects a reasonable trade-off between sensitivity and specificity.

The confusion matrix reveals that the model makes a small number of classification errors, which is expected in real-world scenarios where data is not perfectly separable. Additionally, the ROC curve with an AUC of 0.92 confirms that the model has a strong ability to distinguish between low-risk and high-risk drivers across different classification thresholds.

Overall, the model provides reliable predictions and behaves realistically, making it suitable as a demonstration of logistic regression applied to driver risk assessment.
